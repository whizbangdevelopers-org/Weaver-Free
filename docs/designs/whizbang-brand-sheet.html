whizBANG! Developers LLC — Brand Sheet

This document defines the visual identity for whizBANG! Developers LLC as a company.
The company mark (bomb), wordmark, and color system are shared across all products.
Each product adds its own color and icon to the product layer; this sheet defines the company layer only.
1. Primary Logo — Full Lockup
The primary logo is the wordmark with an oversized "!" whose dot is the bomb — matching the website logo. The "!" is two parts: teardrop stroke (top, towers above "BANG") + bomb (bottom, replaces the dot). Together they form the exclamation mark. The bomb IS the bang. The fuse spark is product-colored (shown here in neutral green; each product tints it to their color).

Key insight from the website logo: The bomb IS the dot of the "!" — not a separate element below it. The blunt stroke bar (wide top, tapers slightly, rounded bottom — not a sharp point) sits above; the bomb sits below-left as the dot. Together they form the exclamation mark. The fuse curves up-right from the bomb's neck collar, spark pointing away from the wordmark. "Developers" is letterspaced and sits below "BANG" in muted green. The digital product version (Inter font) is a deliberate modernization of the website's decorative 3D serif treatment.

4. Company Mark — Bomb
The bomb is the company's visual mark. A round black body with a lit fuse. The fuse spark color changes per product — that's the bridge between company and product identity. Shown here at full detail (72px+).

12. Usage Rules
✓ Do
  • • Use the bomb below "!" in hero/marketing contexts
  • • Use the bomb inline (beside wordmark) in compact contexts
  • • Tint the fuse spark to the current product's color
  • • Simplify the bomb at small sizes per the size guide
  • • Maintain the green company frame (borders, dividers)
  • • Keep "whiz" italic and "BANG" bold — always
  • • Use Inter as the wordmark font — no substitutes
  • • Place the bomb in footer, login, about, and splash
✗ Don't
  • • Place the bomb in product headers or navigation (product mark territory)
  • • Use green as a product color (green is company-only)
  • • Rotate, skew, or distort the bomb
  • • Change the bomb body colors (only the fuse spark changes)
  • • Stack the bomb above the wordmark (it goes below "!" or beside)
  • • Use the bomb without the wordmark in non-avatar contexts
  • • Make "whiz" bold or "BANG" italic — the contrast is the brand
  • • Mix product colors into the company frame (borders stay green)
13. Two-Layer Architecture
Every whizBANG! product has two visual layers. The company layer (this brand sheet) is shared. The product layer is unique per product. They never conflict because they own different UI regions.
The fuse spark is the bridge. It lives on the company mark (bomb) but carries the product color. This is the only element that crosses the boundary between the two layers.
